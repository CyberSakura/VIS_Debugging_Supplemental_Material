# Supplemental Material — Debugging as Evidence-Driven Reasoning

This repository contains the supplemental materials for:

> **Debugging as Evidence-Driven Reasoning: Visualization Opportunities in Data-Intensive Programming.**
> Yongbo Chen, Yan Zhu, and Rebecca Faust. IEEE VIS 2026 Short Papers.

The materials document the qualitative analysis behind the paper and are provided to support transparency and reproducibility of the study's methodology.

## Contents

## Study overview

We conducted semi-structured interviews with nine practitioners whose work involves data processing, analysis, or validation, spanning a range of roles, domains, and programming languages. Each interview lasted approximately 30–45 minutes and was audio-recorded with consent. The study was reviewed and approved by our institution's Institutional Review Board (IRB).

## How the codebook was derived

We analyzed the interview transcripts using inductive thematic analysis. Two coders independently performed open coding and met regularly to compare interpretations, discuss disagreements, and refine code definitions until reaching consensus. Through iterative axial and selective coding, related codes were grouped into seven categories, five of which (Task-related Difficulty, Strategy, Information-Seeking, Attitude, Need/Desire) form the analytic themes reported in the paper; the remaining two (Background/Context, Experience) are descriptive categories used to characterize participants. From these themes, we identified three cross-cutting findings, each recurring across multiple themes — the basis for the theme-to-finding mapping included with the codebook.

## A note on the interview scope

This study is scoped, by design, to debugging support through visualization and intelligent (e.g., LLM-based) assistance, as reflected in its title. The interview protocol therefore refers to visualization tools and AI assistance where it asks participants to reflect on their tool use and on what future support they would find useful. The opening questions about debugging experiences and information-seeking were kept open-ended so that participants could describe their workflows in their own terms before tool- and AI-related topics were introduced.

## A note on anonymization

To protect participant confidentiality under our IRB approval, the examples shown in the codebook are **illustrative paraphrases**, not verbatim quotes. We are unable to release the underlying coded interview excerpts, as they contain identifying details (employers, products, and clients) that our IRB approval requires us to keep confidential. The codebook, theme-to-finding mapping, and interview protocol provided here document the coding scheme and analytic process without exposing this information.
